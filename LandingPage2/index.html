Sherlock Holmes

Genius detective

Sherlock Holmes, deep in thought, puffs on his pipe, surrounded by swirling smoke, as he unravels mysteries with his keen mind.

Sherlock Holmes Biography:

1854 - Born of Sherlock Holmes on January 6, into a respectable family in London, England.

1860-1872 - Holmes' education at several schools, standing out for his intelligence and observation skills from an early age.

1872-1876 - Holmes attends the University of Oxford, where he studies chemistry, philosophy and logic, among other subjects. During this time, he begins to develop his deduction and observation skills.

1877-1880 - Holmes begins his career as a private detective in London, quickly gaining fame for his ability to solve seemingly insoluble cases.

1881 - Holmes meets Dr. John Watson, with whom he forms a firm partnership. Together, they solved a series of challenging cases, winning an enviable victory in the city.

1887 - Publication of the first Sherlock Holmes story, "A Study in Scarlet", introducing Holmes and Watson to the public.

1891 - Holmes investigates the famous "Case of the Hound of the Baskervilles", which becomes one of his most notable and challenging cases.

1893 - Holmes faces his arch-nemesis, Professor Moriarty, in "The Final Problem", culminating in a fight to the death at Reichenbach Falls, where both are apparently killed.

1894-1901 - Holmes' "hiatus" period, during which he is considered dead. However, Holmes spends this time traveling and going on various adventures, as documented in "The Adventure of the Empty House".

1901 - Holmes returns to London, revealing that he survived his fight with Moriarty. He resumed his detective career and continued to solve requested cases alongside Watson.

Until his “death” in “The Final Problem”, Sherlock Holmes continues to be one of the most renowned and revered detectives in literature. His deductive skills, intelligence, and scientific methods shaped the mystery genre and made him an enduring cultural icon.
